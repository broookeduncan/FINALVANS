About Vans

Pictured to the left: Paul Van Doren

In the year 1966, Vans, formerly known as the Van Doren Rubber Company was born. It all started in the land of dreams, the state of California. Two brothers named Paul and James Van Doren were wanting to create footwear that would be sold directly to California customers without having to wholesale to retail stores. The brothers partnered with two friends named Gordon Lee and Serge Delia and its doors re-opened on March 16, 1966.

Originally, Vans shoes were purchased, then made ready for same-day pickup. The original make of the shoe was for skateboarders. This is because the shoes had a rugged and sticky sole that was perfect for keeping skateboarders from falling off their board. Now, Vans can be worn as an every-day shoe, consisting of over 12 different styles!

Although both brothers helped create the famous skate brand, Paul Van Doren is the one who made the company what it is today. Paul passed away on May 6, 2021 and left the company to his son, James Van Doren.
